Arduino UNO Layout

Here is a breakdown of the Arduino UNO layout and the purpose of its pins and features:

Analog Reference

Used for external reference voltage for analog inputs.

Digital Ground

Provides a ground connection for digital circuits.

Digital Pins

Used for general input/output, PWM, or serial communication.

Power Pins

Provide power supply options for external components.

Reset Button

Resets the microcontroller.

USB Port

Used for programming and powering the board.

Microcontroller Specifications

  • Microcontroller: ATmega328P
  • Digital I/O Pins: 14 (6 PWM)
  • Analog Input Pins: 6
  • Flash Memory: 32 KB
  • SRAM: 2 KB
  • EEPROM: 1 KB

Important Notes

  • Use pinMode(), digitalWrite(), and digitalRead() for digital pins.
  • For PWM output, use analogWrite() on supported pins.
  • Avoid exceeding 40 mA per pin to prevent damage.
